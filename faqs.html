Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about university admissions, APS scores, and using this tool.

What is an APS score?
The Admission Point Score (APS) is a system used by South African universities to evaluate applicants. Your matric subject results are converted into points, and the total points determine your APS score. Each university and program will have a minimum APS requirement.
How is my APS score calculated?
APS is calculated by assigning points to your matric subject levels (e.g., Level 7 = 7 points, Level 6 = 6 points, etc.). The total points from your best 6 or 7 subjects (excluding Life Orientation) are summed up to give your final APS. Specific calculations may vary per university.
What if my APS score is too low for my desired course?
You can explore alternative options such as bridging courses, higher certificates, or diplomas. You may also rewrite certain matric subjects to improve your levels. This app provides study tips for improvement.
When should I apply to universities?
Application periods vary by university, but generally, applications open in April/May and close between June and September of the year preceding your desired enrollment. Always check specific deadlines for each institution and program.
Do I need to apply for financial aid separately?
Yes, financial aid (like NSFAS or university-specific bursaries) usually requires a separate application. Apply as early as possible, often concurrently with your university application.
How do I use this tool?
Enter your subjects and levels, then click "Get Recommendations" to see which universities you qualify for. The tool will also suggest ways to improve your APS score.
What are popular programs at South African universities?
Popular programs include Engineering, Medicine, Law, Commerce, Education, and Health Sciences. Each university has its own strengths and specialties.
Who can I contact for more help?
Contact the admissions office of your chosen university, or reach out to us via the contact form on the main page.
